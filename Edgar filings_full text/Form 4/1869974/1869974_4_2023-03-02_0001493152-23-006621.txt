        <footnote id="F1">In connection with the closing on February 14, 2023 (the &quot;Closing&quot;) of the business combination (the &quot;Business Combination&quot;) between Aesther Healthcare Acquisition Corp. (&quot;AHAC&quot;) and Ocean Biomedical Holdings, Inc., formerly known as Ocean Biomedical, Inc. (&quot;Legacy Ocean&quot;), pursuant to that certain Agreement and Plan of Merger, dated August 31, 2022 and amended on December 5, 2022, between AHAC, Aesther Healthcare Sponsor, LLC (the &quot;Sponsor&quot;), AHAC Merger Sub, Inc. (&quot;Merger Sub&quot;), Legacy Ocean, and Dr. Chirinjeev Kathuria, AHAC changed its name to Ocean Biomedical, Inc. (such post-Business Combination entity, the &quot;Issuer&quot;).</footnote>
        <footnote id="F2">Pursuant to the Issuer's Third Amended and Restated Certificate of Incorporation filed in connection with the Closing of the Business Combination, the Issuer completed a reclassification exempt under Rule 16b-7 (the &quot;Reclassification&quot;). In accordance with the Reclassification, each share of the Issuer's Class A Common Stock, par value $0.0001 per share (the &quot;Class A Common Stock&quot;), was reclassified on a one-for-one basis into a share of the Issuer's common stock, par value $0.0001 (the &quot;Common Stock&quot;).</footnote>
        <footnote id="F3">These shares were originally among the 2,625,000 shares of AHAC's Class B Common Stock, par value $0.0001 per share (the &quot;Class B Founder Shares&quot;), that the Sponsor owned prior to the Business Combination. In connection with the Closing of the Business Combination, the Class B Founder Shares automatically converted into shares of the Issuer's Class A Common Stock on a one-for-one basis, and the Class A Common Stock was then reclassified on a one-for-one basis into Common Stock in accordance with the Reclassification.</footnote>
        <footnote id="F4">In accordance with an instruction letter to the Sponsor's transfer agent (the &quot;Instruction Letter&quot;), the Sponsor instructed the transfer agent to transfer 2,625,000 shares of Common Stock to certain of the Sponsor's members for no consideration (such transaction, the &quot;Transfer&quot;). As part of the Transfer, the Reporting Person acquired 80,000 shares of Common Stock from the Sponsor.</footnote>
        <footnote id="F5">Simultaneously with the closing of the AHAC's initial public offering on September 17, 2021, the Sponsor purchased 5,411,000 warrants (the &quot;Private Placement Warrants&quot;) from AHAC in a private placement at a price of $1.00 per warrant, pursuant to the terms of the Private Placement Warrants Purchase Agreement between AHAC and the Sponsor dated September 14, 2021 (the &quot;Private Placement Agreement&quot;). Upon the Reclassification that was effected in connection with the Closing of the Business Combination, each share of Class A Common Stock underlying the Private Placement Warrants was reclassified into a share of the Issuer's Common Stock.</footnote>
        <footnote id="F6">According to the original terms of the Private Placement Agreement, each Private Placement Warrant was exercisable to purchase one share of the Issuer's Class A Common Stock at an exercise price of $11.50 per share. The terms of the Private Placement Warrant were set forth in the Warrant Agreement, dated September 14, 2021, between AHAC and Continental Stock Transfer &amp; Trust Company, LLC (the &quot;Warrant Agreement&quot;). The Private Placement Warrants become exercisable 30 days after the Closing of the Business Combination.</footnote>
        <footnote id="F7">On February 28, 2023, in accordance with the Instruction Letter, the Sponsor instructed its transfer agent to distribute these Private Placement Warrants to the Reporting Person.</footnote>
        <footnote id="F8">The Private Placement Warrants expire on the earlier to occur of (i) February 14, 2028, the date that is five years after the Closing of the Business Combination, (ii) at 5:00 p.m., New York City time on the Redemption Date (as defined in the Warrant Agreement), and (iii) the liquidation of the Trust Account (as defined in the Warrant Agreement).</footnote>
